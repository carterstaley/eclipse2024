Your source for information about the 2024 North American solar eclipse.

WARNING

Never look directly at the sun without protective eye gear, except during the brief total eclipse (and even then, with caution).

What to Bring With You

If you're traveling to a destination in the path of totality, or just stepping out your front door, you'll want to be prepared. Here's a few things to bring with you to make the most of the eclipse.

Protective Eye Gear

Looking at the sun without protection can cause permanent eye damage. To view the moments leading up to totality and afterwards requires special glasses. Regular sunglasses will not work.

Your glasses must block 100% of UV rays, 100% of infrared rays, and 99.999% of visible light.

Sunscreen

You'll probably be spending a lot of time looking up at the sun, so it's a good idea to pack a bottle of sunscreen to protect your face from harmful UV rays.

A Weather App

Even a single cloud passing in front of the sun for a few minutes will obstruct the view of this celestial alignment.

Make sure you have a good weather app installed in your phone, and one that shows cloud cover. You may want to move at a moments notice if you see some clouds rolling in.

Snacks!

Standing out in the sun all day can be exhausting and dehydrating. Make sure you pack plenty of water and high-protein snacks to keep you alert and ready.
