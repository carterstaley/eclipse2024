Your source for information about the 2024 North American solar eclipse.

WARNING

Never look directly at the sun without protective eye gear, except during the brief total eclipse (and even then, with caution).

Welcome!

On April 8th, 2024, the moon will pass directly in front of the sun and cast a shadow across North America. The path the shadow creates is called the path of totality. It will travel across Mexico, through Texas, over the midwest, finally ending over Maine and part of eastern Canada.

Eclipse 2024 is your one-stop-shop for everything you need to know to prepare yourself to view this once-in-a-lifetime phenomenon.

What does it look like?

If you're in a viewing location that's in the path of totality, you will experience a total solar eclipse.

Viewing the sun through special protective eyewear, you will see a disk slowly moving in front of the sun (that's the moon). As the moon moves to completely cover the sun, you will experience a few brief moments of totality.
